29

1 the words of the covenant commanded Moses to make with the children of Israel in the land of Moab beside the covenant which he made with them in Horeb 2 And Moses called unto all Israel and said unto them, Ye have seen did before your eyes in the land of Egypt unto Pharaoh and unto all his servants and unto all his land 3 The great temptations which thine eyes have seen the signs and those great miracles 4 hath not given you an heart to perceive and eyes to see and ears to hear unto this day 5 And I have led you forty years in the wilderness your clothes are not waxen old upon you, and thy shoe is not waxen old upon thy foot 6 Ye have not eaten bread neither have ye drunk wine or strong drink that ye might know your God 7 And when ye came unto this place Sihon the king of Heshbon and Og the king of Bashan came out against us unto battle and we smote 8 And we took their land and gave it for an inheritance unto the Reubenites and to the Gadites and to the half tribe of Manasseh 9 Keep therefore the words of this covenant and do them, that ye may prosper in all that ye do 10 Ye stand this day all of you before your God your captains of your tribes your elders and your officers all the men of Israel 11 Your little ones your wives and thy stranger in thy camp from the hewer of thy wood unto the drawer of thy water 12 That thou shouldest enter into covenant thy God and into his oath thy God maketh with thee this day 13 That he may establish thee to day for a people he may be unto thee a God as he hath said unto thee, and as he hath sworn unto thy fathers to Abraham to Isaac and to Jacob 14 Neither with you only do I make this covenant and this oath 15 that standeth here with us this day before our God not here with us this day 16 For ye know how we have dwelt in the land of Egypt and how we came through the nations which ye passed by 17 And ye have seen their abominations and their idols wood and stone silver and gold 18 Lest there should be among you man or woman or family or tribe whose heart turneth away this day our God to go serve the gods of these nations lest there should be among you a root that beareth gall and wormwood 19 And it come to pass, when he heareth the words of this curse that he bless himself in his heart saying I shall have peace though I walk in the imagination of mine heart to add drunkenness to thirst 20 will not spare him, but then the anger and his jealousy shall smoke against that man and all the curses that are written in this book shall lie shall blot out his name from under heaven 21 shall separate him unto evil out of all the tribes of Israel according to all the curses of the covenant that are written in this book of the law 22 So that the generation to come of your children that shall rise up after you, and the stranger that shall come from a far land shall say when they see the plagues of that land and the sicknesses hath laid 23 the whole land brimstone and salt burning it is not sown nor beareth nor any grass groweth therein, like the overthrow of Sodom and Gomorrah Admah and Zeboim overthrew in his anger and in his wrath 24 Even all nations shall say done thus unto this land the heat of this great anger 25 Then men shall say Because they have forsaken the covenant God of their fathers which he made with them when he brought them forth out of the land of Egypt 26 For they went and served other gods and worshipped them, gods whom they knew he had not given 27 And the anger was kindled against this land to bring upon it all the curses that are written in this book 28 rooted them out of their land in anger and in wrath and in great indignation and cast them into another land this day 29 The secret our God revealed unto us and to our children for ever may do all the words of this law
